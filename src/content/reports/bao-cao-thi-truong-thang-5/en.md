The market continued to push prices higher throughout this period, particularly after May 8, when the Ivorian government maintained restrictions on exporters purchasing domestic raw cashew nuts or processing existing contracted shipments.

Several lower-priced IVC contracts signed earlier for April shipment remained undelivered, increasing concerns among Vietnamese buyers as prices continued to rise.

Cashew kernel buyers in Europe and the United States remained cautious, although the current upward price trend was increasingly confirmed through purchases from major buyers.
